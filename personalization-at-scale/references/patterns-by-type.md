# Personalization Patterns by Type

Group prospects by the personalization angle found, then apply the matching pattern. Each section gives a sample first line, why it works, and how to organize prospects in that category.

## Congratulations

Prospects with recent achievements, funding, promotions, or launches to congratulate.

### Series B Announcement

> "Congrats on the Series B! $25M is awesome validation. With that kind of growth, [likely pain point you solve]..."

Why this works:
- Timely (announced within 2 weeks)
- Shows you are paying attention
- Transitions naturally to their likely needs during scale-up

Similar prospects: Series A raises, promotions to VP, product launches.

### New Role

> "[Name], saw you recently joined [Company] as [Title]. Congrats! First 90 days in a new role are always [crazy/exciting/challenging]. I imagine [relevant challenge]..."

Why this works:
- New job means open to new vendors/solutions
- First 90 days is a common pain point
- They are likely evaluating tools now

## Observations

Prospects who posted content, made comments, or showed activity that gives an opening.

### LinkedIn Post About a Topic

> "Loved your take on [topic] in your recent post. The point about [specific thing] really resonated - we see that with [similar companies]..."

Why this works:
- Shows you read their content
- Specific callback to what they said
- Connects their thought to your expertise

## Mutual Connections

Prospects with 1st or 2nd degree connections to reference.

> "Hi [Name], I noticed we're both connected with [Mutual Connection]. She and I worked together at [Company] and when I saw you're the [Title] at [Company], thought I should reach out about [topic]..."

Why this works:
- Mutual connection creates instant credibility
- Feels less cold
- Can potentially get a warm intro

Alternative approach: ask the mutual connection for an intro first, then reference in follow-up ("[Mutual] suggested I reach out...").

## Company News

Companies with recent press mentions, launches, or announcements.

### Company Expansion

> "[Name], saw [Company] is opening 3 new offices across [region]. That kind of expansion is exciting but usually creates [specific challenge you solve]..."

Why this works:
- Shows company-level awareness
- Ties expansion to a likely pain point
- Timely and relevant

## Hiring Signals

Companies with job postings that indicate growth, tech changes, or priorities.

> "Noticed you're hiring 5+ engineers according to your LinkedIn jobs page. Scaling eng teams that fast usually creates [specific problem you solve]..."

Why this works:
- Job postings are public but not everyone notices
- Hiring means growth means budget
- Can infer pain points from the roles they are hiring

| [Company 2] | 5 DevOps | Infrastructure growth | Cloud cost management | High |

## Tech Stack Changes

Companies that recently adopted or announced technology changes visible through job descriptions, case studies, or tech blogs.

> "I saw in a recent job posting that you're migrating to [Technology]. We help companies during that transition with [specific problem]..."

Why this works:
- Migration means change means potential for new vendors
- Shows technical awareness
- Timely opportunity

## Thought Leadership

Prospects who appeared on podcasts, webinars, published blogs, or spoke at events.

> "Really enjoyed your appearance on [Podcast Name]. Your point about [specific insight] was spot-on - we actually help companies with exactly that..."

Why this works:
- Flattering (they will appreciate you listened/read)
- Can reference specific talking points
- Shows genuine interest

## Shared Background

Prospects who share alma mater, previous company, location, or interest with you or someone on your team.

### Same University

> "Go [Mascot]! Saw you graduated from [University] too. I was there [years]. Anyway, I'm reaching out because [value prop]..."

Why this works:
- Instant rapport with alums
- Shared identity boosts trust
- Breaks the ice

### Same Previous Company

> "Small world - I saw you worked at [Company] from [years]. I was there around the same time in [department]. [Transition to business]..."
