# Champion Scoring Framework

## What Makes a Great Champion

Champion profile (all five must be present):
- Has Pain: directly affected by the problem the solution solves
- Has Power: can influence the decision or control budget
- Has Gain: personally benefits when you win (promotion, bonus, easier work)
- Is Accessible: reachable, relationship can be built
- Is Willing: open to new solutions and vendors

Distinguish three roles:
- Champion: actively sells for you internally, has skin in the game
- Coach: helpful but passive, gives intel but will not advocate
- Blocker: opposes the solution (favors incumbent, risk-averse, loses if you win)

## Scoring Dimensions (0-10 each)

1. Role Relevance - how directly the role relates to the solution
2. Influence Level - ability to affect the buying decision
3. Accessibility - reachability, warm intro possible
4. Change Agent - track record of adopting new solutions
5. Personal Stake - personal gain if you win
6. Engagement Potential - likelihood to respond to outreach

## Total Champion Score (0-60)

- 50-60: Ideal champion candidate
- 40-49: Strong potential champion
- 30-39: Possible champion with work
- Below 30: Not likely to champion

## Warning Signs (Not Actually a Champion)

- Too agreeable: says yes to everything but never acts; just being polite
- Cannot get you to others: promises introductions that never happen
- Does not know the process: cannot describe how decisions get made or who holds budget
- Not actually affected: speaks about the problem abstractly, no personal examples
- No skin in the game: no personal KPIs tied to the problem, just researching for someone else

When these appear: keep the contact in the loop (may be useful later), find the real champion who feels the pain, and do not invest in coaches posing as champions.
