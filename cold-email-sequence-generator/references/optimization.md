# Optimization: A/B Testing, Metrics, Segmentation, Pro Tips

## A/B Testing Strategy

Test variables, in priority order.

Subject lines (test first):
- Question vs. statement
- Generic vs. personalized
- Short (3-5 words) vs. long (8-12 words)
- Curiosity vs. value prop

Email body:
- Length: short (50 words) vs. medium (100 words)
- CTA: link vs. question vs. time slot
- Bullets vs. paragraph format
- Social proof: stats vs. names vs. quotes

Sending time:
- Morning (9-11 AM) vs. afternoon (2-4 PM)
- Tuesday vs. Wednesday vs. Thursday
- Recipient's time zone (test if worth the complexity)

Sample A/B test (Email 1):
- Version A: Curiosity subject + short email (50 words) + question CTA
- Version B: Value subject + medium email (100 words) + meeting-time CTA

Send to 100 prospects: 50 get A, 50 get B. Wait 48 hours, measure open and reply rates. Send the winner to the remaining list.

## Sequence Performance Metrics

| Metric | Good | Great | Exceptional |
|--------|------|-------|-------------|
| Email 1 Open Rate | 35-45% | 45-55% | 55%+ |
| Email 1 Reply Rate | 3-8% | 8-15% | 15%+ |
| Sequence Reply Rate | 8-15% | 15-25% | 25%+ |
| Positive Reply % | 40-50% | 50-70% | 70%+ |
| Meeting Booked % | 1-3% | 3-6% | 6%+ |

Drivers of high reply rates: highly personalized opening line; clear value prop in the first two sentences; social proof from similar companies; low-friction CTA; sent at an optimal time; clean formatting (no images, minimal links).

Drivers of low reply rates: generic template language; overly salesy tone; no personalization; vague value prop; lengthy paragraphs; broken links or poor formatting.

## Segmentation Strategy

By industry: change case studies to the same industry; adjust pain points to be industry-specific; use industry terminology.

By company size:
- Startup: speed, agility, ROI focus
- Mid-market: scalability, efficiency
- Enterprise: security, compliance, integration

By role:
- Executive: strategic outcomes, revenue impact
- Practitioner: time savings, ease of use
- Technical: architecture, integrations, specs

By intent signal:
- Hot leads: shorter sequence, faster cadence
- Warm leads: standard 7-email sequence
- Cold leads: longer nurture sequence

## Pro Tips

1. The 3-second rule: prospect should understand value in the first 3 seconds of reading.
2. One CTA only: give one clear next step.
3. Mobile-first: 50%+ of emails open on mobile; keep it scannable.
4. No attachments: use links; attachments trigger spam filters.
5. Real reply-to: use your actual email, not no-reply@, and reply.
6. Personalization tokens: use sparingly; obvious automation kills trust.
7. The P.S. works: postscripts get read; use for a secondary CTA.
8. No formal unsubscribe needed for 1-to-1 prospecting.

What to avoid:
- Spam trigger words: "Free", "Limited time", "Act now", "$$$$", "Make money", ALL CAPS, excessive exclamation points.
- Design no-nos: images (especially logos), HTML-heavy templates, multiple font colors/sizes, long links (use shorteners).

## Setup Checklist

- [ ] Sender email has good deliverability (SPF, DKIM, DMARC)
- [ ] Sender email is warmed up (sent successful emails recently)
- [ ] List is cleaned (no invalid emails)
- [ ] Personalization variables all filled
- [ ] Links tested and tracked
- [ ] CRM integration working
- [ ] Reply handling process in place
- [ ] Unsubscribe process ready
- [ ] A/B tests configured
- [ ] Time-zone sending enabled
- [ ] Daily send limits set (avoid spam flags)
